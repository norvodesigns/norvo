## 16.0 — The Doctrine: Mobile Is Authored First, Not Reduced

Most of the web treats the phone as the desktop site shrunk until it fits. Norvo treats the phone as **the first place every moment is designed to live.** The thesis of File 00 — *websites can be environments you travel through* — is not weaker on a small screen; it is more intimate. The visitor holds the place in one hand, turns it in the light with their wrist, and moves through The Ascent with their thumb. A phone is closer to the body than a monitor will ever be, and Norvo designs for that closeness deliberately.

This file therefore never uses the phrase "simplified for mobile," and no file in this bible ever may (File 18, §18.3). The correct word, fixed in the glossary, is **re-choreographed**: every interaction, animation, navigation pattern, and transition is *deliberately re-designed for touch and small screens* — exact gestures, exact layout shifts, exactly what is re-timed, how depth and parallax adapt, what is added and what is set aside, and why the result remains premium. A re-choreography can *remove* a thing the finger cannot do (there is no hover on a phone), but it must *replace* it with a thing the finger can — never merely delete it and leave a gap.

The doctrine rests on five governing laws. Every entry in the catalog below obeys all five.

### 16.0.1 — The Five Laws of Mobile

1. **Touch-first, not touch-also.** The primary input on a phone is the thumb. Targets are sized generously for a fingertip and spaced so adjacent controls are never mis-tapped. Hover — which does not exist under a finger — is never required to discover or reach anything; every affordance is visible at rest and acts on the very first tap. The desktop "invitation" stage (the warm-toward-violet hover of File 10, §10.7) collapses into the touch's "acknowledgment" stage: finger-down *is* the response, immediate and unmistakable.

2. **The Covenant is answerable one-handed.** The five questions of the Usability Covenant (File 00, §0.6) — where am I, how do I navigate, how do I contact Norvo, how do I start a project, how do I view the work — are answerable on a phone with one thumb, from anywhere, at any moment in The Ascent or the site. The persistent frosted top strip and the repeated footer guarantee it. Immersion is never a trap; the visitor can skip ahead or return at any time with one obvious tap.

3. **Depth survives by changing instrument.** The desktop reads depth through pointer position; the phone reads it through the **gyroscope and the scroll**. Wherever a File specifies pointer-driven **Parallax** (File 01, §1.3), the phone substitutes **device-tilt parallax** and **scroll-driven parallax** of equal intention — near layers answer the wrist and the thumb more than far layers, selling the same real front-to-back depth through a different sense. Tilt-parallax is always subtle and slow, never disorienting, and it softens to a gentle still state under reduced-motion preference (File 17). The trinity of intent — depth, motion, intention — is fully present on the phone; only the input changes.

4. **Re-timed, never re-meant.** The phone canvas is smaller, held closer, and crossed by a thumb rather than swept by a pointer. Motions are therefore **re-timed** — a touch shorter so a small screen does not feel sluggish, a touch slower where a sweep of gradient light would otherwise feel hurried on a tiny field — but never **re-meant.** A **Settle** still settles, a **Reveal** still reveals, a **Bloom** still blooms, the **Warp** still warps. No motion is downgraded to a different named behavior on the phone; it is the same behavior on a different stage.

5. **The device is part of the architecture.** The phone's own hardware — the status region, the Dynamic Island or notch, the home indicator, the on-screen keyboard, the safe-area insets at every edge — is treated as part of the place, not an obstacle laid over it. The Norvo chrome is engineered to clear the system clock and camera cutout with a calm margin of breathing room, so the wordmark and menu affordance are never crowded (File 10, §10.3.2). When the keyboard arrives, content above it re-flows calmly to make room; it never jumps (File 10, §10.6.1). Every edge respects the device's safe margins so nothing the visitor must read or tap is ever clipped by a rounded corner or hidden behind a home indicator.

### 16.0.2 — The Gesture Vocabulary (Fixed for the Whole Site)

So the visitor learns the phone's behavior once, the entire experience uses one small, consistent set of touch gestures. No moment invents a private gesture the visitor must discover.

- **Tap** — *commit and acknowledge.* The universal action: open a destination, choose a chip, fire a button, advance a step. Finger-down delivers the acknowledgment (deepened color, a contained glow blooming from the contact point); on lift the action fires. This is the workhorse gesture of the entire site.
- **Vertical swipe (scroll)** — *travel.* The primary motion through The Ascent and down every page. On the homepage, the upward swipe is the visitor's forward progress through the five eras; the **Shift** at each Seam and the **Warp** are paced to it exactly, so the visitor feels they are *driving* the journey with their thumb, never watching it autoplay. A downward swipe always travels back the way they came.
- **Horizontal swipe** — *browse a set.* Used only where content is an explicit set to page through (a card carousel, the eras viewed as a filmstrip in a condensed overview). Never used for primary forward progress, so it can never be confused with the vertical travel of The Ascent.
- **Long-press** — *reveal optional detail.* A calm, secondary gesture, never required to complete any primary task. A long-press on a project card surfaces its badges and a one-line description; a long-press is never the only way to reach anything in the Covenant. It is a gift for the curious, not a toll.
- **Device tilt** — *feel depth.* The wrist's motion drives every parallax the desktop drove with the pointer (Law 3). Tilt is never required to act; it only ever deepens atmosphere. It is always gentle, always optional in effect, and always calmed under reduced-motion.
- **Pinch / spread** — *deliberately reserved.* Norvo does not repurpose the system zoom gesture for storytelling; pinch-to-zoom remains the operating system's accessibility behavior so a low-vision visitor's expectation is never broken. No Norvo moment requires a pinch.

**One gesture, one meaning, everywhere.** A tap never means two different things in two different moments; a swipe-up is always "forward / down through the place." Consistency is what keeps an immersive site from ever feeling like a puzzle.

## 16.1 — The Consolidated Mobile Catalog

What follows is the section-by-section catalog. For each major moment of the experience it states the precise mobile gestures, the layout shift from desktop, what is re-timed, how depth and parallax adapt, and the felt quality — and it agrees with the mobile subsection already written in that moment's own file. Where this file and another file appear to disagree, the other file's own mobile subsection and File 10 win for component behavior; this catalog is the consolidated view, never a contradiction.

The catalog follows the visitor's path: the homepage Ascent (the Intro, the four historical eras, the Time Warp, and the arrival in The Observatory), then the world beyond the Observatory's windows (the Spaceport) and the walled prototypes, then the persistent navigation, then each post-homepage page in canon order.

### 16.1.1 — The Intro (The Opening of The Ascent)

*The threshold of The Ascent, fully specified in File 02.*

The very first moment a visitor arrives at Home, before Era 1 has fully formed, is the threshold of The Ascent.

- **Gestures:** A single upward swipe begins forward travel. Nothing else is required; the visitor is never asked to "tap to start" (that would be game-menu language, forbidden by File 00, §0.5). The frosted top strip and its menu affordance are already present from the first instant, so the Covenant is answerable before the journey even begins.
- **Layout shift:** The opening composition stacks to a single centered column sized to the phone's portrait field, with the wordmark held in the top strip clear of the status region and Dynamic Island.
- **Re-timing:** The first **Reveal** of the opening type is paced a touch slower than desktop, so the visitor registers the calm before the climb on a small, close screen.
- **Depth:** Minimal here by design — the intro is near-monochrome Graphite and Archive White (File 01, §1.2, Era 1). The faint depth that exists answers a gentle tilt of the wrist, introducing the gyroscope's role quietly before depth matters.
- **Felt quality:** Stillness held in the hand. The phone feels like a quiet archive opened on the visitor's palm.

### 16.1.2 — Era 1 · Documents (Temperament: Austere)

*The Typed Artifact (File 01, §1.1); Monochrome (File 01, §1.2); fully specified in File 03.*

- **Gestures:** Upward swipe to read and to advance. The single live forward affordance — the lone **Norvo Violet** point in a monochrome world, the first sign of life (File 01, §1.2, Era 1) — is a generous thumb target carrying a slow violet **Pulse**, so the one thing the visitor may act on is unmistakable without any hover to discover it.
- **Layout shift:** The single narrow reading column of the desktop (File 01, §1.4, Era 1) becomes the phone's full comfortable measure with wide, calm safe-area margins. The implied strict baseline rhythm is preserved exactly; the column simply fills the portrait width.
- **Re-timing:** The type's "being typed or developed" animation — characters and lines settling in sequence at a calm reading cadence — is paced identically in feel but begins the instant a line scrolls into view, so reading and forward travel stay in step with the thumb. Once set, type is perfectly still, as on desktop.
- **Depth:** Essentially flat by intent (Era 1 material is lit-flat paper, File 01, §1.5). Tilt produces no parallax here; depth has not yet entered the story, and the phone honors that restraint rather than inventing motion.
- **Felt quality:** A single typed page resting in the hand, in a silent room. The lone violet point is the first warmth the thumb is drawn toward.

### 16.1.3 — Era 2 · Pages (Temperament: Composed)

*The Composed Column (File 01, §1.1); First Warmth (File 01, §1.2); fully specified in File 04.*

- **Gestures:** Upward swipe travels through the now-designed layout. Links and the active heading, newly marked in **Norvo Violet**, are thumb targets; the first touch of **Observatory Gold** (a key initial, a rule under a title) appears exactly as a small, precious mark — never enlarged or made tappable merely to fill the screen.
- **Layout shift:** This is the most significant structural re-choreography of the early eras. The desktop's **true multi-column editorial grid** (File 01, §1.4, Era 2) cannot survive at portrait width without crowding, so the columns **re-flow into a single reading column in deliberate reading order** — the same hairline rules, the same disciplined margins and gutters re-proportioned, the same hierarchy, simply stacked. The composition's *logic* (margins, rules, considered hierarchy) is fully preserved; only its horizontal arrangement folds to vertical. This is re-choreography, not reduction: nothing is dropped, the order is authored, and the page still reads as "designed."
- **Re-timing:** Columns that "fade up in reading order" on desktop arrive as stacked blocks fading up in that same reading order as they scroll into view — a brief **Reveal** per block, paced to the thumb's travel.
- **Depth:** The faintest print-like separation between layered elements (File 01, §1.5, Era 2) answers a gentle tilt as the lightest parallax — the first whisper of the gyroscope carrying depth, kept barely perceptible to match the composed-but-still temperament.
- **Felt quality:** A finely typeset page that has learned to hold itself together in one hand. Warmth has entered; the gold mark feels precious because it is rare.

### 16.1.4 — Era 3 · Interfaces (Temperament: Operational)

*The Labeled System (File 01, §1.1); Color as Signal (File 01, §1.2); fully specified in File 05.*

- **Gestures:** Upward swipe travels; **tap** now does real work, because this is the era the web "begins to *do* things." Controls, toggles, and live values respond to the first touch with immediate state change — the desktop's hover-preview of a control collapses into the tap's acknowledgment. Where the desktop shows a value ticking on hover-reveal, the phone ticks it on entry into view or on tap, never requiring a pointer to hover.
- **Layout shift:** The desktop's **modular grid of panels, rows, and fields** (File 01, §1.4, Era 3) re-flows into a single vertical stack of full-width panels. Within a panel, the labeled rows — label left, value right-aligned in its field — are preserved, because the monospace alignment of numerals is part of the era's meaning (File 01, §1.1, Era 3); the phone keeps each panel's internal column logic and simply stacks the panels. Dense desktop control clusters become comfortably spaced stacked controls, each a clean thumb target.
- **Re-timing:** State-change motion — values that tick or roll a short mechanical count, labels that brighten when active (File 01, §1.1, Era 3) — fires on tap with no perceptible delay, the immediacy the operational temperament demands. **Violet** focus glows mark the active control exactly as on desktop.
- **Depth:** Panels carry the lightest elevation (File 01, §1.5, Era 3); a gentle tilt drifts nearer panels a hair more than farther ones, giving the stack of interface surfaces real, quiet front-to-back layering through the gyroscope rather than through a pointer.
- **Felt quality:** A precise instrument the thumb operates. Color now means *state* under the finger — violet is *on*, and the visitor feels the page responding to touch.

### 16.1.5 — Era 4 · Experiences (Temperament: Immersive)

*The Spatial Word (File 01, §1.1); Color as Light (File 01, §1.2); fully specified in File 06.*

This is the era where the phone's re-choreography of depth matters most, because Era 4 is built from depth, **Parallax**, and **Drift** (File 01, §1.3).

- **Gestures:** Upward swipe is now *forward glide through a deep space* — the **Drift** (File 01, §1.3) is paced to the thumb, so the visitor drives their own advance into the scene. **Device tilt** is the headline gesture here: tilting the phone shifts the layered depths against one another, the nearest words and forms answering the wrist most, the deep field answering least, exactly reproducing the desktop's pointer-and-scroll **Parallax** through the gyroscope. A large, near display phrase parallaxes boldly; atmospheric type far in the field barely moves.
- **Layout shift:** The desktop's grid has **dissolved into depth** (File 01, §1.4, Era 4), so there is little column to re-flow — the composition is already spatial. On the phone the front-to-back layers are re-proportioned to portrait: the few large, near words are sized to command the narrow field without clipping, and supporting type recedes smaller and farther behind them. The boldest scale contrast of the whole journey survives, re-fit to the tall canvas.
- **Re-timing:** The forward **Drift** is tied 1:1 to the upward swipe; **Parallax** is tied 1:1 to tilt and scroll, with the faintest lag on the farthest layers to sell distance (File 01, §1.3). Headlines easing in "from slightly behind the picture plane" do so as they enter the scrolling field, settling near with a **Settle**. Nothing autoplays past the visitor's control.
- **Depth:** Full, real, atmospheric — violet and golden light, luminous Archive White edges against Graphite depth, gentle haze in the deep field (File 01, §1.5, Era 4). The phone renders this volume and lets the wrist move through it. Under reduced-motion the tilt-parallax softens to a gentle still depth and the **Drift** becomes a calm cross-fade, the *meaning* of "I am inside something now" surviving without the movement (File 01, §1.3; File 17).
- **Felt quality:** Being inside something held in the hand — the visitor leans the phone and the world leans with it. This is the deepest immersion before the climb's peak, and the thumb and wrist together are what produce it.

### 16.1.6 — The Time Warp (The One Climactic Seam)

*The Warp (File 01, §1.3); the emotional peak of The Ascent (File 00, §0.3); fully specified in File 07.*

The single most intense motion of the journey, used exactly once, on the phone as on desktop.

- **Gestures:** The visitor *triggers and paces the Warp with the continuing upward swipe* — the same forward gesture that has carried them through every era now carries them through its peak, so the climax feels driven, not inflicted. The accelerate-then-decelerate of the **Warp** maps to the thumb's continued travel: as the visitor keeps swiping forward, the world speeds up into the rush; as the rush crests and resolves, deceleration carries them into the calm arrival regardless of further input, so they are never trapped mid-rush waiting (the Covenant's "never an interactive movie they must wait out," File 00, §0.5).
- **Layout shift:** The streaking, gradient-lit motion of the **Signature Gradient** (violet flowing into gold, File 01, §1.2/§1.3) fills the full portrait field rather than a wide frame, so the rush wraps the visitor's whole held screen. The composition is re-fit so the streaks read as forward depth on a tall canvas, never as horizontal scan lines.
- **Re-timing:** The Warp is paced a touch tighter than a large desktop so the intensity reads on a small, close screen without dragging — but its three-act feel (build → rush → decelerating arrival) is preserved exactly. The single sonic peak (File 01, §1.6) crests and lands with the visual, where sound is enabled and unmuted.
- **Depth:** Maximal — the only moment the experience approaches a true sense of velocity. Tilt is *suspended* during the Warp's rush so the visitor's wrist cannot fight the climactic forward motion; depth here is the engineered acceleration, not the gyroscope. As the Warp decelerates into The Observatory, tilt-parallax returns gently, handing depth back to the wrist for the settled place ahead.
- **Felt quality:** The peak in the palm — a held breath of speed and light that resolves, decelerating, into arrival. The visitor's own thumb carried them through it, and that authorship is what makes it exhilarating rather than overwhelming.

### 16.1.7 — The Observatory (The Future — Places, Temperament: Harmonious)

*The Luminous Readout (File 01, §1.1; File 10, §10.1.2); Harmony of all four colors (File 01, §1.2); believable future architecture, never outer space (File 00, §0.5; File 18, §18.1); fully specified in File 08.*

The visitor decelerates out of the Warp into The Observatory — the settled future-state place that the whole site beyond Home also lives in (File 10, §10.0).

- **Gestures:** Travel calms. Upward swipe now moves the visitor *through a settled place* rather than up a climb; **tap** operates the instruments (the components of File 10); **device tilt** drives the Observatory's gentle, quiet depth. The pace of every gesture's response is the most refined on the site, matching the harmonious temperament.
- **Layout shift:** The loose, breathing, slightly architectural grid (File 01, §1.4, Places) re-flows to a single calm vertical column of abundant space on the phone — the opposite of Era 1's tightness, re-proportioned to portrait. Wayfinding labels align to the architecture as on desktop; everything has room to breathe within the safe-area margins.
- **Re-timing:** Type **settles and glows** (File 01, §1.1, Places) with the faintest slow **Signature Gradient** shimmer across key focal words, re-timed to read calmly on a small field — the shimmer is slowed a touch so it never feels hurried on a tiny readout. Idle type is perfectly calm; active type warms on tap.
- **Depth:** Gentle, real, and quiet (File 10, §10.2) — the navigation nearest, content panels in a calm middle, the architectural ground farthest, felt through the lightest tilt-parallax and elevation, never an Era-4-scale plunge. The drama of Experiences has resolved into the poise of Places, and the wrist's motion through that poise is small and serene.
- **Felt quality:** Arrival, held in the hand. A lit, calm, finished place the visitor inhabits with their thumb — all four colors in balance, the **Signature Gradient** at peace on the few true focal moments.

### 16.1.8 — The Spaceport (The World Beyond the Observatory's Windows)

*The living vista seen through the Observatory's windows — scenery, never a destination, never interactive (File 09); a believable working future at the scale of a civilization, never outer space and never a spaceship (File 00, §0.5; File 09).*

The Spaceport is the view *out* the tall windows of The Observatory: the evidence that the room the visitor stands in is real, opening onto an enormous, working future that recedes past the edge of the glass. It is the deepest layer of the entire experience and the calmest. It is **seen, never entered** — the visitor never travels to it, boards anything, or operates anything within it; every interactive element lives in the *interior* (File 09, §9.4). On the phone this distinction is held exactly: the visitor's thumb always acts on the room; the wrist and the scroll only ever let them *marvel at* the world outside.

- **Gestures:** None of the Spaceport's own. The Spaceport answers only **vertical swipe (scroll)** and **device tilt**, and only ever with truthful depth — never to a tap, a long-press, or any pointing (File 09, §9.4). Scrolling down through the Observatory's content travels the view across the vista; a gentle tilt of the wrist lets the visitor look around the held window a few degrees. The visitor never taps the view and is never invited to — there is nothing out there to operate, and the phone makes that as plain as the desktop does, so the Covenant's "the room is where I act, the window is where I marvel" is unmistakable on touch.
- **Layout shift:** The desktop's **wide, panoramic window** becomes a **tall portrait slot** onto a deep world (File 09, §9.6). A broad horizon scanned left-to-right would smear into illegibility at portrait width, so the mobile window is reconceived to sell scale through *depth into the distance* rather than width: the apron near and low, the structures rising, the transit systems threading across, the far field and sky stacked above and receding upward. The horizon still sits high in the frame so built ground dominates over sky, and the downward-and-outward angle is preserved — the visitor still looks *down and out* onto infrastructure, never up at a void (File 09, §9.1). The window frame stays fixed as Graphite architecture; only the world beyond it moves, so the visitor always reads themselves as *inside, looking out.*
- **Re-timing:** On desktop the whole panorama is present at once; on the phone the vista is **revealed through the vertical scroll** (File 09, §9.6). Early in the descent the nearer bands (apron and structures) fill the tall frame; as the visitor continues, the view eases toward the far field and sky, so the *sense of scale accumulates* as they travel — the world keeps revealing more of itself the further the thumb carries them, which on a small screen conveys vastness more powerfully than one static wide shot could. The **ambient life** is re-timed for the narrower frame so it always holds *one or two* moving transit craft and a small scatter of slowly shifting working-lights — alive, never busy. The far-field atmospheric shimmer and the rare high-craft arcs are preserved but appear less frequently, matched to the narrower window so each remains a quiet, noticed event. The **time-of-day evolution** — the slow Shift from a cool, clear, architectural calm toward a warm, golden richness, with the **Signature Gradient** rising low across the sky where the world's light meets the horizon (File 09, §9.3) — is preserved in full and is, if anything, *more* rewarding on a phone: across a single continuous scroll-through the visitor glances back to find the horizon glow deepened and the world warmer than they left it.
- **Depth:** The Spaceport is the deepest **Parallax** in the experience, and on the phone it is driven by both instruments (Law 3). The five depth bands move at their canonical differential rates **tied to the vertical scroll** — apron most, structures less, transit less still, far field barely, sky least, with the faintest lag on the farthest reaches (File 09, §9.2/§9.4). A **gentle device-tilt parallax** is added as the only new mobile behavior: angling the held phone shifts the nearer bands a few degrees against the far ones, giving the window a living look-around quality the desktop pointer cannot — a *hint* of depth, never a swooping camera, and always subordinate to the scroll (File 09, §9.6). Depth is, if anything, *more* felt on the phone, because the bands slide past a tall, intimate frame held close to the eye. Under reduced-motion the scroll-parallax, the tilt response, the transit motion, and the time-of-day arc all soften into a calm, deep, beautifully composed *still* vista — the same five bands, the same four-color harmony, the scale and arrival carried entirely by composition and color (File 09, §9.7; File 17).
- **Legibility and the Covenant on mobile:** On the smaller, brighter-held screen, content legibility is even more sacred. Whenever Norvo's content sits over or beside the window, the Spaceport behind it **dims and quiets more decisively than on desktop** — the moving lights soften, the parallax slows, the haze deepens slightly — so the words always read with full clarity against a calm ground (File 09, §9.6). The interior reflection on the glass is kept very faint so it never competes with content. The interior, the navigation, and every call to act remain fixed, obvious, and one thumb-tap away, anchored to the room and never floated over the busy vista. Nothing essential is ever delivered *only* through the Spaceport; for assistive technology it carries a clear text-equivalent of its meaning — that beyond the windows lies a vast, working future of structures, transit, and lit infrastructure receding to the horizon — described as scenery, never as something to operate (File 09, §9.7).
- **Felt quality:** The quiet awe of standing in a finished, warm room held in the hand and realizing, through the tall window, that the world it belongs to is *enormous, alive, and already being built* — scale that is real, never a movie set, never a galaxy of stars. The thumb travels the view, the wrist tilts the window a few degrees, and the future extends past the edge of the glass farther than the visitor can see the end of.

### 16.1.9 — The Prototype Walls (Aurora and Strata)

*Aurora and Strata are walled-off universes with their own branding (File 18, §18.1, the Prototype walling rule); the Projects page frames their entry and exit (File 00, §0.9; File 11). Aurora is the luxury private-spaceflight concept; Strata is the layered real-depth parallax real-estate concept (File 18, §18.1).*

A prototype is a self-contained brand universe reached **from the Projects archive** (File 11) — not the Spaceport of §16.1.8, which is Norvo's own scenery beyond the Observatory windows. Crossing into a prototype is the moment the visitor steps out of Norvo's own canon and into another studio's finished world. This catalog governs only the **Norvo frame** around a prototype — the entry from Projects, the always-visible return, and the exit transition — never the prototype's internal mobile choreography, which belongs to that prototype's own universe.

- **Gestures:** A **tap** on a prototype entry in the Projects archive opens it; a single, always-visible **return affordance** (a calm tertiary action carrying the Norvo wordmark's route back) lets the visitor leave the walled universe with one thumb at any time. The visitor is never stranded inside a prototype; the Covenant's "how do I get back" is answerable on the phone throughout. Inside the wall, the prototype answers only its own gestures — they are not specified here.
- **Layout shift:** Crossing the wall is a witnessed transition (a **Reveal** into a **Settle**, File 10, §10.10): the Norvo Observatory chrome recedes and the prototype's own world arrives. **Inside the wall, the prototype follows its own mobile design — not Norvo's** (File 18, §18.1: "Never restyle a prototype's internals to match Norvo"). Aurora and Strata each carry their own palette, typography, and touch behavior; the four-color / Geist-Mono system and this doctrine govern only the Norvo frame around them.
- **Re-timing:** The entry and exit transitions across the wall are re-timed to the phone exactly as page-to-page travel is (File 10, §10.10) — a brief recede-and-arrive, never a flash.
- **Depth:** The Norvo frame's depth follows Observatory rules (tilt-parallax, gentle elevation); the depth *inside* a prototype is its own and is not specified here.
- **Felt quality:** Stepping through a clearly marked door into another studio's finished world, and always knowing — with one visible thumb-reachable affordance — how to step back out into Norvo.

### 16.1.10 — The Navigation System (Persistent Chrome)

*The spine of the Covenant (File 00, §0.6); the mobile navigation specified in File 10, §10.3.2.*

- **Gestures:** A **tap** on the menu affordance — three short stacked Geist Mono rules tracked to feel like a precise instrument glyph, not a generic hamburger — opens the full menu. A **tap** on a destination travels there; a **tap** on the close glyph (two crossed Geist Mono rules) or on any visible sliver of the place behind the menu closes it without choosing.
- **Layout shift:** The desktop's slim full-width horizontal bar (File 10, §10.3.1) becomes a slim top strip carrying the wordmark (Home) at left and the menu affordance at right. Opening it brings a **full-height Archive White frosted panel** sliding down from the top edge, listing all five destinations — **Home / Projects / Services / Contact / Start a Project**, canon order (File 18, §18.1) — as large, generously spaced thumb targets, with **Start a Project** as a full-width **Signature Gradient** primary button at the bottom of the stack.
- **Re-timing:** The panel arrives over about one calm beat and comes to rest with a **Settle** — no bounce; the place behind it softens with a gentle blur and slight dim so the menu reads as the nearest layer (File 10, §10.3.2). Closing reverses the motion exactly.
- **Depth:** The menu is the nearest layer; the dimmed, blurred place behind it sits farther back — a clear two-plane depth the visitor reads instantly. The bar follows the **transparent-at-top / frosted-Archive-White-when-scrolled** behavior and **never becomes fully opaque** (File 10, §10.3.1; and the standing project rule that the mobile nav stays transparent-at-top or frosted white when scrolled, never fully opaque). It clears the status region and Dynamic Island with a calm margin (Law 5).
- **Felt quality:** The whole place is one flat list of five, always one obvious tap away. The visitor never hunts through nested layers to reach a primary destination — the menu is never a maze (File 10, §10.3.2).

### 16.1.11 — The Home Page (Hosting The Ascent)

## 16.3 — How the Mobile Doctrine Holds Together

If a team is ever unsure whether a phone behavior belongs, run it against the doctrine:

1. **Is it touch-first** — sized and spaced for the thumb, acting on the first tap, never requiring a hover that a finger cannot perform?
2. **Is the Covenant answerable one-handed** — can the visitor still always tell where they are, navigate, contact Norvo, start a project, and view the work, with one thumb, from here?
3. **Does depth survive by the right instrument** — pointer-parallax re-tied to device tilt and scroll, subtle and slow, calming under reduced-motion?
4. **Is it re-timed, never re-meant** — the same named motion (Settle, Drift, Parallax, Reveal, Bloom, Shift, Warp, Pulse) on a different stage, never downgraded to a different behavior?
5. **Is the device part of the architecture** — safe areas, status region, Dynamic Island, keyboard, and home indicator all honored as part of the place?
6. **Is it a re-choreography, not a reduction** — did anything removed get a touch-native replacement, and is the word "simplified" nowhere in the thinking?
7. **Does it agree with the moment's own file** — does this behavior match the mobile subsection already written in File 10 and the era files, never contradicting them?

Seven yeses, or it is not first-class mobile.
