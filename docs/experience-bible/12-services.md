# Norvo Designs — Experience Bible
## File 12 · The Services Page (Capability as a Place You Walk Through)

> The Services page is where Norvo stops *claiming* mastery and starts *demonstrating* it. It is the proof-of-craft room of the site — the place a serious client comes to understand what Norvo does, how deeply Norvo does it, and why it matters to their business. It inherits The Observatory's Future UI System (File 10) in full: the Luminous Readout type, the four-color harmony, instrument-grade material and light, and the eight named motions. Read File 00, File 01, File 10, and File 18 first. Where this file describes *what the Services page holds and how it is arranged*, File 10 governs *how every component within it looks and behaves*; if they appear to disagree, File 10 wins.

---

## 12.0 — The Governing Idea: A Page That Performs Its Own Argument

Most services pages are a grid of six cards, each with an icon, a heading, and a paragraph. They *list* capability. The visitor reads "Interactive Experiences — motion that tells your story," nods, and remembers nothing, because a paragraph about motion is not motion, and a card about depth has no depth.

The Norvo Services page refuses the list. Its single governing idea: **every claim is demonstrated by the very surface that makes it.** The module that promises "motion that tells your story" tells a small story in motion as the visitor approaches it. The module that promises "luxury at lightning speed" arrives faster and lighter than any other. The module about mobile reshapes itself, in front of the visitor, into a phone. The page is not *about* Norvo's capability; the page *is* a controlled demonstration of it. The medium is the argument here exactly as it is on the homepage — only now the visitor is already standing inside The Observatory, and the demonstrations are the calm, lit instruments of a finished place rather than the rising drama of The Ascent.

This is the page's discipline and its restraint at once: each of the six modules is allowed to *perform its own promise* — but only its own, only once, and only in the refined, harmonious Observatory temperament. Nothing here is a tech demo or an Awwwards stunt. Each demonstration is small, exact, legible, and in service of a single understandable claim. Spectacle that does not clarify a capability is cut.

The visitor leaves the Services page able to answer three questions without effort: *What can Norvo do? How well? And what would it do for me?* The first is the six modules. The second is the felt quality of how each module behaves. The third is the impact stats, presented not as decoration but as a closing argument the visitor *feels* before they read.

## 12.1 — The Arrival: Entering the Services Hall

The visitor reaches Services from the persistent navigation, from a footer link, or by jumping straight in from the outside world. However they arrive, the page-to-page travel of File 10 §10.10 carries them in: the prior content recedes a short distance and softens back, the navigation bar holds as the building's persistent chrome, and the Services content arrives from a hair behind the picture plane and settles forward (a **Reveal** into a **Settle**). The violet "you are here" marker slides onto **Services** in the navigation *during* the move, so location is never lost.

### 12.1.1 — The Header (The Statement of Capability)

The top of the page is a calm, spacious Archive White field — the lit interior of The Observatory — holding a single confident statement of what this room is for. It is set in the Luminous Readout: a page title in Geist Mono, medium-to-heavier weight, exactly as large as it needs to be and no larger, with abundant space around it. The title states the room plainly — *the capabilities of the studio* — in Norvo's declarative, unhurried voice. Beneath it sits one quiet supporting line in regular weight and quieted Graphite, establishing the frame: that everything here is built, not templated; designed with depth, motion, and intention.

A single focal word or short phrase in the header — the one true crown of the view — may carry the faint slow **Signature Gradient** shimmer drifting across it like light moving on brushed metal (File 10 §10.1.2). This is the one place the gradient lives in the header; it is never sprayed across the supporting copy. Graphite and Archive White carry the rest of the header in calm; violet and gold are withheld here, held in reserve for the modules and the stats below, so the visitor's first impression is poise rather than display.

As the header settles, the six modules below it are still gathering — present first as the calm Archive White skeleton of File 10 §10.8, their exact shapes lit by the faintest slow Signature-Gradient sweep, so the visitor sees the room *lighting up* rather than a blank wait. The modules then resolve into their shapes with a gentle **Reveal**, in reading order, top to bottom — never popping in out of sequence.

### 12.1.2 — The Wayfinding Promise

Before the visitor scrolls a pixel, the Usability Covenant is intact. The navigation bar overhead carries the full destination set with **Services** marked in violet. The page makes clear, in its very first screen, that what follows is six capabilities and a closing argument — there is a quiet sense of the room's extent, a felt "there is structure here, and an end to it," so the visitor never fears an endless scroll. And the single most important commercial action of the entire page — the route to **Start a Project** — is present from the first moment in the navigation's primary button and is repeated as the page's closing invitation (see §12.7). A client who has seen enough at any point can act immediately; the demonstrations are a gift, never a toll.

## 12.2 — The Architecture of the Six Modules

The six modules are the body of the page. They are **not** a flat grid of identical cards. They are a calm vertical sequence of capability stations — each a distinct, full-attention station the visitor travels *through*, in canon order:

> **01 Custom Website Design → 02 Website Redesigns → 03 Mobile Optimization → 04 Interactive Experiences → 05 Performance Optimization → 06 Brand Experience Design.**

The sequence is deliberate and is itself a small argument: it opens with the foundational act (building from nothing), moves through transformation and reach (redesign, mobile), rises into the studio's signature craft (interactive experiences), sharpens into discipline (performance), and resolves into wholeness (brand across every touchpoint). The visitor descends through capability the way they ascended through eras on the homepage — as a guided, intentional path with clear forward motion, never an open field to wander.

### 12.2.1 — The Shared Module Anatomy

Every module, however its demonstration differs, shares one anatomy so the visitor learns the page's grammar once:

- **The index numeral** — the module's two-digit number (01–06) set in Geist Mono, leaning on the monospace's native numeral strength, aligned exactly. It is the wayfinding spine: the visitor always knows which of six they are in. The numeral of the *current* module warms to violet with a faint **Pulse** as that module holds the visitor's focus; the others rest in quieted Graphite.
- **The module name** — the canon name (e.g., *Custom Website Design*) in the Luminous Readout, medium weight, exactly as written in canon, never abbreviated or rephrased.
- **The tagline** — the fixed canon line, set in regular weight beneath the name, quieted Graphite: *"Built from the ground up, not from templates"* · *"From outdated to unforgettable"* · *"Premium on every screen"* · *"Motion that tells your story"* · *"Luxury at lightning speed"* · *"Consistency across every touchpoint."* These lines are immutable and appear exactly as canon.
- **The demonstration field** — the surface where that module *performs its promise* (specified per module in §12.3). This is what makes each module unlike the others.
- **One quiet affordance** — where a module invites a deeper look, a tertiary or secondary action (File 10 §10.4) sits beneath it, violet, quiet, and clear. Never more than one per module, and never a competing primary; the page's single primary action is reserved for Start a Project.

### 12.2.2 — The Spatial Layout (Desktop)

On a wide canvas the modules read as **full-width capability stations stacked vertically**, not as a tight six-up card grid. Each module occupies a generous horizontal band with its own breathing room above and below, so the visitor gives one module their full attention at a time. Within a band, the layout alternates its weight side to side as the visitor descends — the demonstration field anchored to one side and the name/tagline/numeral to the other, then mirrored on the next — creating a calm reading rhythm down the page (left-anchored, right-anchored, left-anchored…) that keeps the long descent from feeling like a uniform list. This alternation is gentle and architectural, never zigzag-busy; it is the loose, breathing, slightly-architectural grid of The Observatory (File 01 §1.4), not the dense modular grid of Era 3.

The depth language of File 10 §10.2 governs the stack: the navigation sits nearest, each active module's demonstration field sits in the calm middle layer, and the architectural ground sits farthest. As the visitor scrolls, the lightest **Parallax** gives the page real front-to-back quiet — the demonstration fields drift a hair against their grounds — but never an Era-4-scale plunge. The drama has resolved into poise.

### 12.2.3 — Focus and the "Lit Station" Behavior

Only one module is ever fully "lit" at a time — the one centered in the visitor's view as they scroll. As a module reaches center, its numeral warms to violet, its demonstration field comes fully alive (its motion begins, see §12.3), and its luminous edge strengthens a hair — a clean **Settle** of attention, no bounce. Modules above and below rest in a calm, slightly receded, slightly dimmer state, their demonstrations idle and still. This is the page's focus discipline: the visitor's eye is never split across six competing animations. Capability is demonstrated **one station at a time**, each given its moment, exactly as a fine archive lights only the case you are standing before.

When a module leaves center as the visitor scrolls on, its demonstration eases back to idle (a gentle settle to rest, never an abrupt stop) and its numeral cools to Graphite — handing the violet focus forward to the next. The transition of focus from one station to the next is always gradual and witnessed; nothing snaps alight or dark.

## 12.3 — The Six Demonstrations (Capability Made Visible)

This is the heart of the page. Each module performs its own promise in one small, exact, legible demonstration. Each is described below in one-interpretation precision: what the visitor sees, how it moves, and what claim it proves. All six obey the four-color discipline (Graphite and Archive White carry roughly nine-tenths; violet is the living signal; gold is reserved for value), the Luminous Readout, and the named motions — no fifth color, no second font, no bounce, no flash, no teleport.

### 12.3.1 — Module 01 · Custom Website Design — *"Built from the ground up, not from templates"*

**The demonstration: the page builds itself from nothing.** As this module reaches center, its demonstration field begins empty — a bare Archive White ground with only the faintest implied baseline rhythm. Then structure *constructs* itself in front of the visitor: a single Graphite hairline draws across the field (a foundation line settling into place), a column boundary eases in beside it, a heading block develops into being, a content panel and an image frame **Reveal** in reading order — each element arriving from a hair behind the picture plane and settling forward, assembling into a small, complete, custom page layout. The build is calm and deliberate (one element at a time, in logical construction order), never a frantic flurry. When the layout completes, a small Geist Mono micro-label — *built, not chosen* — settles beside it in quieted Graphite.

**The claim it proves:** the visitor *watches a page be constructed from raw structure outward*, with no template stamped down — the opposite of a layout dropped in whole. Construction, witnessed, is the argument. On idle (module not centered) the field rests as the finished custom layout, still.

### 12.3.2 — Module 02 · Website Redesigns — *"From outdated to unforgettable"*

**The demonstration: a single transformation, witnessed.** This module's field holds one surface that exists in two states. At idle it rests in a "before" state — a flat, crowded, near-monochrome layout, tight and joyless: dense Graphite text on Archive White with no breathing room, no warmth, no light. As the module reaches center, the surface **transforms in place** through a single continuous re-tempering (a contained, small-scale **Shift**, the same gradual re-tempering language as the homepage's era Seams): space opens and loosens, the cramped column breathes outward, the hierarchy resolves, the first warmth enters as a touch of violet on a now-live affordance and a single mark of Observatory Gold crowning the title, and the whole surface settles into an "after" state that is calm, lit, and confident. The visitor watches *the same content* travel from outdated to unforgettable — never a hard cut between two pictures, always a witnessed metamorphosis. A quiet Geist Mono label tracks the move: *before* fading as *after* arrives.

**The claim it proves:** redesign is not replacement; it is transformation. The visitor sees the *journey* from old to new in one breath, which is exactly what Norvo sells. The demonstration loops slowly and calmly when centered (a long pause in the "after" state, then a gentle return to "before" to offer the transformation again), never a restless flicker.

### 12.3.3 — Module 03 · Mobile Optimization — *"Premium on every screen"*

**The demonstration: the layout reshapes itself for the phone, in front of you.** This module's field holds a small wide desktop-proportioned layout at idle. As the module reaches center, the field **re-choreographs itself into a phone**: the wide frame narrows in a smooth continuous **Settle**, the content reflows — columns stacking into a single comfortable column, the navigation condensing into the slim top strip and instrument-glyph menu affordance of File 10 §10.3.2, the type and spacing re-proportioning to the small canvas — until a complete, premium phone layout rests in the field, lit and exact, never a squashed or broken shrink. The reshape is one fluid transformation the visitor watches happen; the layout is clearly *as considered* small as it was wide. A quiet label reads *every screen*.

**The claim it proves:** mobile is first-class, not a fallback — the demonstration literally enacts the re-choreography the bible demands of mobile everywhere, showing premium quality surviving the shrink. (Fittingly, on a phone this module's demonstration runs the transformation *in reverse* — see §12.6 — so the visitor on a phone watches their own canvas being honored.) On idle the field rests as the finished phone layout, still.

### 12.3.4 — Module 04 · Interactive Experiences — *"Motion that tells your story"*

**The demonstration: a small story told purely in motion and depth.** This is the module that most resembles the homepage's own soul, rendered at Observatory calm. Its field holds a small layered scene — a few elements arranged at real front-to-back depths against Graphite space. As the module reaches center, the scene comes alive: foreground elements **Drift** slowly forward, mid-ground and background layers **Parallax** at their own depths as the visitor's pointer (or, on phone, the device tilt) moves across the field, soft Archive White light and a breath of Norvo Violet glow move through the scene, and a single short phrase eases forward from behind the picture plane and settles near — a tiny narrative arc, beginning to settled resolution, told entirely through depth, motion, and light rather than text. It is the trinity of intent demonstrated literally. The Signature Gradient may make its one allowed appearance here as a slow luminous wash through the scene's focal moment — the brand's soul, shown doing exactly what the module sells.

**The claim it proves:** Norvo makes motion that *means* something — depth and movement carrying story, not decoration. The visitor doesn't read about immersive experience; they feel a contained one. The scene's motion is continuous and weighty when centered (a slow living loop, the **Drift** and **Parallax** never resting jittery) and softens to a calm near-still state at idle.

### 12.3.5 — Module 05 · Performance Optimization — *"Luxury at lightning speed"*

**The demonstration: this module arrives faster and lighter than any other.** Performance is proven by behavior, not by claim. Where every other module's demonstration assembles with deliberate, unhurried calm, this module's demonstration **resolves with a single crisp, immediate snap of readiness** — its content is simply, instantly *there*, lit and complete, the instant the module reaches center, with a clean **Settle** so brief it reads as "already arrived." (This is the one place on the entire page where speed itself is the message — and even here it is weighty and confident, never a jarring flash; it lands clean, it does not pop.) The field then holds a calm, exact readout — a small Geist Mono instrument panel showing a value resolving to a fast, clean state, a single luminous gauge filling to "lit" in one smooth, quick **Settle**, the numerals aligned in their monospace columns with instrument precision. A quiet Observatory Gold mark crowns the resolved value — speed presented as *prestige*, value earned.

**The claim it proves:** the visitor *feels* the difference in tempo — this capability behaves faster and cleaner than its neighbors, so "luxury at lightning speed" is demonstrated in the page's own pacing. Performance is the one thing you cannot fake in a demonstration, so Norvo lets the demonstration simply *be* fast. At idle the gauge rests lit and still.

### 12.3.6 — Module 06 · Brand Experience Design — *"Consistency across every touchpoint"*

**The demonstration: one identity, harmonized across many surfaces.** This closing module's field holds several small distinct surfaces at once — a fragment of a site, a card, a small mark, a label, a control — arranged calmly across the field. At idle they sit slightly out of accord, each a touch different in tone and treatment. As the module reaches center, a single wave of harmony passes across them: a slow **Bloom** of the brand system spreading outward from a point — the same Geist Mono treatment, the same four-color roles, the same light — until every surface in the field shares one coherent identity, all four colors resolving into the calm harmony of the Signature Gradient's resolved chord (violet and gold at peace, exactly as the homepage's journey resolves). The visitor watches disparate touchpoints *become one brand* in a single continuous settling. A quiet label reads *every touchpoint, one voice*.

**The claim it proves:** brand consistency is shown as literal visual harmonization across surfaces — the module performs the resolution it promises, and fittingly closes the page on the same note of four-color harmony the entire site resolves toward. The harmonized state holds calm and still at idle; centering re-runs the Bloom of harmony.

### 12.3.7 — The Demonstration Discipline (Governs All Six)

- **One claim, one demonstration, once.** Each module performs only its own promise, in one small legible move. No module borrows another's trick.
- **Legibility never yields to spectacle.** Every demonstration is readable, calm, and clearly understandable as *what it proves*. If a designer cannot state in one sentence which claim a motion proves, the motion is cut.
- **Named motions only.** Every demonstration maps to the Motion Vocabulary (File 01 §1.3): Settle, Drift, Parallax, Reveal, Bloom, Shift, Pulse. No bounce, no overshoot, no flash, no teleport.
- **Four colors only, in role.** Graphite and Archive White carry the demonstrations; violet marks what is alive; gold is reserved for value (the resolved value in Performance, the warmth of harmony in Brand). The Signature Gradient appears at most once per view, in the Interactive Experiences module's focal moment or the Brand module's resolution — never in two demonstrations at once on screen.
- **Idle is calm.** A module not in focus is still or near-still — the page is never six competing animations. Demonstrations live only for the centered station.

## 12.4 — The Impact Stats (The Closing Argument You Feel)

After the six modules, the page resolves into its argument: the four impact statistics — **50ms first impression · 75% credibility from design · 88% will not return after bad UX · 2× conversion lift.** These are the fixed canon stats, presented exactly as written. They are not a footnote and not a decorative band of big numbers; they are the page's *closing case* — the felt translation of "good design is not a luxury, it is the business." And critically, **the stats are not merely shown — they are staged so the visitor feels each one before they finish reading it.**

### 12.4.1 — The Stats Field

The stats live in their own calm, distinct band below the six modules — a spacious field, set apart by generous space so the eye understands "the demonstrations are done; here is what they are worth." The four stats sit in a calm, even arrangement (a confident architectural row or two-by-two on wide canvases), each in the Luminous Readout: the **figure** large and exactly weighted in Geist Mono, the **explanatory line** small, in quieted Graphite, beneath it. This is the one place gold earns real prominence — the figures are the *value* the whole page has been building toward, and Observatory Gold marks them as such, the prestige of arrival made literal in the closing numbers.

### 12.4.2 — Each Stat as a Felt Argument

As the stats band reaches the visitor's view, each figure **counts up** to its value — a short, clean mechanical roll of the monospace numerals (the Era-3-native value tick of File 01 §1.1, resolved into Observatory calm: a measured count, never a slot-machine spin), settling on the final number with a **Settle**. But each stat is also *staged* so its meaning lands physically:

- **50ms first impression** — the figure resolves almost instantly, faster than the eye can track it begin — the number is simply *there*. The stat about how fast a first impression forms *forms that fast*. The visitor feels the speed of judgment in the speed of the number's arrival.
- **75% credibility from design** — as the figure settles, the field around it visibly composes: roughly three-quarters of the surrounding surface warms from bare Archive White into a designed, lit, considered state, leaving a quieter remainder — the visitor *sees* three-quarters of credibility being built by design itself.
- **88% will not return after bad UX** — as this figure settles, the great majority of a small implied field of presence quietly recedes and departs — a calm, sober drift away, never alarmist or flashing — leaving a sparse remainder. The cost of bad experience is felt as *most of something leaving and not coming back.* This is the page's one note of consequence, delivered with restraint: a quiet emptying, not a warning.
- **2× conversion lift** — the figure resolves and a single quiet measure visibly doubles — one calm bar or value growing to twice its length in a smooth **Settle**, the gold of value crowning the result. The upside is felt as growth, the closing note of warmth after the sobering one.

The sequence is intentional: speed, then credibility built, then the cost of getting it wrong, then the reward of getting it right — a small four-beat argument that ends on gain. Each stat's staging is small, exact, and legible; none is a spectacle. The four figures are the only place on the page where gold leads, and that is the point — *this is the value, and Norvo lets you feel it.*

### 12.4.3 — Stats Discipline

The figures and their lines are fixed canon and never altered, rounded differently, or rephrased. The staging never overwhelms the number — the figure is always instantly readable; the felt argument is an *accompaniment* to the number, not a replacement for it. The stats animate once on arrival and then rest calm and still (each figure holding its final value), never looping restlessly. Under reduced-motion preference (File 17, and §12.8 below), the count-up and each felt staging soften to a calm direct presentation of the final figure with its meaning stated plainly — the *argument* survives without the movement.

## 12.5 — The Closing Invitation (From Capability to Action)

The page does not end on numbers; it ends on a door. After the stats, the Services page resolves into a single, calm, confident invitation to act — the bridge from "here is what we can do, and what it is worth" to "let us do it for you."

- **Composition:** A spacious closing field holding one short, declarative Geist Mono line in Norvo's voice — calm, certain, never hyped — inviting the visitor to begin. Beneath it sits the page's single **primary button**, *Start a Project*, carrying the **Signature Gradient** (File 10 §10.4.1): the one focal moment where the brand's resolved chord lives as a fill in this view, with its faint slow shimmer at rest. This is the most important commercial action on the page, and it is given the page's one true crown.
- **The discipline of singularity:** Up to this point in the page body, the Signature Gradient has appeared only as the header's faint shimmer and at most once inside a single demonstration; gold has led only in the stats. The closing button is the page's resolution — the one primary action, unmistakable, the answer to "what does this page most want me to do." There is no competing primary anywhere else in the view.
- **The footer beneath** repeats the Usability Covenant per File 10 §10.11 — the wordmark, the full navigation, the contact email to **norvodesigns@gmail.com**, and the three socials (Instagram, X, Facebook) — so even a visitor who scrolled past the invitation can still reach any destination, contact Norvo, or start a project from the settled foundation of the page.

## 12.6 — The Mobile Services Page (First-Class, Re-Choreographed)

Mobile is not a smaller Services page; it is the Services page re-choreographed for the thumb and the small canvas, with equal intention. Every promise the desktop page makes, the phone keeps — re-timed, re-laid-out, and re-gestured, never "simplified."

### 12.6.1 — Layout and Flow

- **The header** stacks: title, then the single supporting line, both full-width with generous space, the focal word carrying the same faint Signature-Gradient shimmer. The skeleton-then-**Reveal** arrival behaves identically, re-proportioned to the small canvas.
- **The six modules** stack into a single comfortable column in canon order — there is no side-to-side alternation, because there is one column; instead the rhythm comes from generous spacing between stations and the lit-station focus behavior. Each module is full-width, with its numeral, name, canon tagline, demonstration field, and single quiet affordance stacked vertically, generously spaced as an unambiguous, thumb-reachable station.
- **The stats band** stacks the four figures vertically (or a calm two-by-two where the canvas comfortably allows), each full-width, the figure large and the line beneath it, gold leading exactly as on desktop.
- **The closing invitation** centers, with the *Start a Project* primary button full-width and within easy thumb reach (File 10 §10.4.1), and the footer stacks into its calm vertical list (File 10 §10.11) so every destination, the contact email, and Start a Project remain one thumb away at the very bottom.

### 12.6.2 — The Lit-Station Behavior on Mobile

The "one module lit at a time" focus discipline is *more* important on a phone, where only one or two stations are on screen at once. As the visitor scrolls a module to the comfortable center of the viewport, that module becomes the lit station — its numeral warms to violet with a faint **Pulse**, its demonstration comes alive, its edge brightens — while the partial modules above and below rest calm and idle. Scrolling carries the violet focus from station to station gradually, exactly as on desktop; the demonstration of a module that scrolls away eases back to idle. The visitor moves *down through* capability, one lit station at a time, never confronted by six things moving at once on a small screen.

### 12.6.3 — The Demonstrations on Mobile

Each demonstration is re-gestured for touch, preserving its claim:

- **01 Custom Website Design** — the build-from-nothing assembly plays on arrival at center, re-timed to the small canvas; the **Parallax** of construction (where present) is re-tied to device tilt rather than pointer.
- **02 Website Redesigns** — the before→after **Shift** plays on arrival and loops slowly; identical in meaning, re-timed.
- **03 Mobile Optimization** — on a phone this demonstration is most powerful run **in reverse**: the field begins as a wide desktop layout and re-choreographs into a phone layout that matches the very device the visitor is holding — the canvas honoring its own canvas. The reshape plays on arrival at center.
- **04 Interactive Experiences** — the layered scene's **Parallax** is driven by **device tilt** instead of pointer position (File 10 §10.5.3's tilt-parallax language): the visitor gently tilts the phone and the scene's depths respond, delivering the same real front-to-back story. The **Drift** and focal-phrase arrival play on centering. Tilt-parallax is subtle and slow, never disorienting.
- **05 Performance Optimization** — arrives with the same crisp immediacy on centering; the gauge and aligned numerals resolve in one quick clean **Settle**, proving speed in the page's own behavior on the phone exactly as on desktop.
- **06 Brand Experience Design** — the harmony **Bloom** across the small surfaces plays on centering, resolving into the four-color harmony.

### 12.6.4 — The Stats on Mobile

Each figure counts up and performs its felt staging as it scrolls into view, re-timed for the small canvas: 50ms resolves near-instantly, 75% composes its surrounding field, 88% enacts its sober quiet departure, 2× doubles its calm measure. The staging is slowed a touch where needed so it reads calmly on a small screen (matching File 10 §10.8's mobile re-timing), and gold leads the figures identically. Each animates once on its arrival, then rests still.

### 12.6.5 — Touch Grammar Throughout

Every affordance on the page obeys the shared interaction grammar of File 10 §10.7 in its mobile form: no hover stage; finger-down delivers an immediate, contained acknowledgment (a deepened color and a soft glow blooming from the contact point); on lift, the action fires. The single quiet affordance per module, the closing *Start a Project* button, and every footer link are all generously sized thumb targets with clear spacing so adjacent targets are never mis-tapped.

## 12.7 — The Usability Covenant on the Services Page

At every point on this page, on any device, the visitor can answer the five covenant questions of File 00 §0.6 without effort:

1. **Where am I?** — The navigation marks **Services** in violet; the header names the room; the module numerals (01–06) and the lit-station focus tell the visitor exactly which capability they are in and how many remain. The page's structure has a felt extent and a clear end.
2. **How do I navigate?** — The persistent navigation bar (frosted-when-scrolled, never opaque) and the repeated footer navigation are always one obvious action away; the visitor can leave for any destination at any moment.
3. **How do I contact Norvo?** — The Contact destination is in the navigation and the footer; the real email (**norvodesigns@gmail.com**) sits in the footer as a calm tertiary action.
4. **How do I start a project?** — *Start a Project* is in the navigation's primary button from the first screen and is the page's single crowning closing action. Commercial intent is never buried beneath the demonstrations.
5. **How do I view the work?** — The Projects destination is always reachable in the navigation and footer.

The demonstrations are a gift, never a toll: a visitor uninterested in watching them can scroll straight past to the stats and the invitation, or leave for any page, at any time. Immersion is never a trap.

## 12.8 — Reduced Motion, Loading, and Graceful States

The Services page honors File 10 §10.8 (loading), §10.9 (empty states), and File 17 (accessibility) in full.

- **Reduced-motion variant:** Where a visitor prefers reduced motion, every demonstration softens into a calm, equally legible form that *states its claim without the movement.* Module 01's build resolves directly to its finished custom layout with a quiet label confirming it was built, not chosen. Module 02's before→after becomes a gentle cross-fade between the two states with the meaning intact. Module 03 presents the wide and phone layouts side by side as clearly equivalent quality. Module 04's layered scene becomes a calm, still composition with its focal phrase present and its depth shown through layered light rather than motion. Module 05 simply presents its resolved, lit gauge and aligned value — speed shown as a finished readout. Module 06 presents the surfaces already in harmony. The stats present their final figures directly with their meanings stated in plain Geist Mono. The *argument of every module and stat survives* even when its motion is removed — exactly the bible's rule that the meaning of every motion outlives the movement.

Under reduced motion, the sweep softens to a steady glow.

## 12.9 — How the Services Page Holds Together

If a team is unsure whether something on the Services page belongs, run it against the page's discipline:

1. **Does each module perform its own canon promise** — one claim, one demonstration, once — in a move a designer can name in a single sentence?
2. **Is the type Geist Mono in the Luminous Readout**, the canon module names and taglines exactly as written, the numerals aligned in their monospace columns?
3. **Are only the four colors used in their Observatory roles** — Graphite and Archive White carrying the page, violet as the living signal, gold reserved for value and leading only in the stats — and is the Signature Gradient reserved for the header's faint shimmer, at most one demonstration's focal moment, and the single closing *Start a Project* button?
4. **Is only one module lit at a time**, so the page is never six competing animations?
5. **Are the four impact stats exact canon** (50ms · 75% · 88% · 2×), each *felt* before it is finished being read, animating once and then resting calm?
6. **Does every motion map to a named behavior** (Settle, Drift, Parallax, Reveal, Bloom, Shift, Pulse) with no bounce, no flash, no teleport?
7. **Does every demonstration, stat, and affordance define its precise mobile equivalent** — re-timed, re-laid-out, tilt-parallax for pointer-parallax, thumb-sized targets — never "simplified for mobile"?
8. **Is the Usability Covenant intact** — can the visitor always tell which capability they are in, how to navigate, how to contact Norvo, how to start a project, and how to view the work — and does the single primary *Start a Project* action crown the page's close?
9. **Does reduced motion preserve the argument** of every module and every stat through calm, legible, still presentation?

Nine yeses, or it is not the Services page.
