# Norvo Designs — Experience Bible
## File 14 · Start a Project (The Intake)

> The climactic act of the entire site: the moment a visitor stops touring the future of the web and decides to **build with Norvo in it**. Where the homepage's Ascent is the studio's argument and the rest of The Observatory is its evidence, the Intake is the visitor's *answer* — the place where intent becomes a brief. This file describes that six-step passage in full. It inherits the Observatory design language (File 10), the cross-era system (File 01), and the Usability Covenant (File 00), and it never contradicts them. Read File 00, File 01, File 18, and File 10 first.

---

## 14.0 — The Governing Idea: The Commission Room

Every other page in The Observatory invites; the Intake is where the visitor **commits**. It is the only place on the site where Norvo asks the visitor to give rather than receive — to hand over their name, their goals, their taste, their budget, and their trust. The Intake must therefore be the most reassuring, the most dignified, and the most effortless room in the entire place. It is *momentous* — the visitor feels the weight of beginning something real — and yet *effortless* — no step ever feels like work.

We name this room so every team speaks of it the same way: **the Commission Room.** (When this bible says "the Commission Room," it means the future-state Start a Project experience — the six-step Intake as a single guided spatial passage. The page is still always called **Start a Project** in navigation and in all copy facing the visitor; "the Commission Room" is the internal name for its experience.)

The Commission Room is not a form stacked on a page. It is a short, lit corridor of six stations through which the visitor travels — **Project → Vision → Style → Structure → Budget → Review** — each station a calm Observatory surface that asks for exactly one kind of thing, and nothing more. The visitor is never shown all six steps' worth of fields at once; that would be a wall of work. They are shown one station at a time, and they *travel* between them. The journey forward is the same recede-and-arrive travel that moves the visitor between pages of the site (File 10, §10.10) — so beginning a project feels like continuing the same single journey the homepage began, not like dropping into a separate "form page."

Three promises govern every decision in this room, and every team holds them:

- **The visitor never feels they are filling out a form.** They feel they are *briefing a studio* — telling Norvo about the thing they want to build, in an order that mirrors how a real conversation with a designer would unfold.
- **The visitor never fears losing their work.** Because answers persist as they go (File 10, §10.6.4), the room quietly, constantly reassures them their brief is held — even if they leave and return days later.
- **The visitor always knows exactly where they are in the six, how far remains, and that they may move backward as freely as forward.** Progress is never a trap; the door back is always as lit as the door forward.

The Commission Room obeys the Observatory temperament — **harmonious** (File 01, §1.0): the system held loosely and confidently, all four colors in calm balance, Geist Mono in the Luminous Readout treatment, motion at its most refined. Nothing here shouts. Beginning a project should feel like the most natural, most settled act on the site — because it is the act the whole site was built to invite.

## 14.1 — The Architecture of the Room (Constant Across All Six Steps)

Before the stations themselves, the structure that holds them — the parts of the Commission Room that never move, so the visitor learns the room once and is then free to think only about their brief.

### 14.1.1 — The Persistent Navigation and the Always-Open Doors

The slim primary navigation bar (File 10, §10.3) holds at the top of the Commission Room exactly as on every page — **Home / Projects / Services / Contact / Start a Project**, with *Start a Project* shown as the active location in Norvo Violet (warmed label, thin violet rule, the faint violet **Pulse** of "you are here"). The Usability Covenant is never suspended for the sake of the brief: at any moment, in any of the six steps, the visitor can leave for Projects, jump to Contact, or return Home in one obvious action. Committing to a brief is never a tunnel the visitor cannot exit. Because answers persist, leaving and returning costs them nothing — the room remembers.

The persistent footer (File 10, §10.11) sits at the base of the Commission Room as everywhere, repeating the full navigation, the Contact email (**norvodesigns@gmail.com**), and the three socials. The visitor is never stranded at the bottom of a step.

### 14.1.2 — The Station Surface

Each of the six steps is presented on a single calm **station surface** — a bright Archive White panel floating a hair above the deeper Graphite architecture of the place, with crisp soft-rounded corners, the lightest luminous edge, and the softest elevation shadow (the Observatory card material, File 10, §10.5). The station is wide and unhurried; its fields and choices breathe inside generous space. Only one station is ever present at a time. The deep Graphite architecture of the surrounding place is faintly sensed beyond the station's edges — the visitor is in a lit room, not on a blank document.

Above the station's fields sits the step's own quiet identity: a small uppercase tracked-out Geist Mono **station label** naming the step (PROJECT, VISION, STYLE, STRUCTURE, BUDGET, REVIEW — the canon step names, each set as its own word), and beneath it a single calm line of plain Geist Mono **station copy** — one sentence, declarative and warm, telling the visitor in human terms what this station is for. The station copy is the studio's voice speaking to the visitor at the start of each part of the conversation. It never hypes, never over-explains; it sets the visitor at ease and lets them begin.

### 14.1.3 — The Progress Constellation (The Step Indicator)

At the top of the station, beneath the navigation, sits the **progress constellation**: the calm horizontal sequence of six Geist Mono labels — **Project · Vision · Style · Structure · Budget · Review** — that is the visitor's map of the whole Intake. This is the step indicator specified in File 10, §10.6.4, given its full form here. It is the single most important wayfinding instrument in the Commission Room, and it answers "Where am I in the six? How far remains?" at a glance, always.

Its color logic is exact and never ambiguous:

- **The current step** is marked in **Norvo Violet** — its label warms to violet, a thin violet rule sits just beneath it with a soft luminous edge, and a faint violet **Pulse** breathes under it: *you are here.*
- **Completed steps** each carry a small **Observatory Gold** confirmation mark beside the label — a quiet, precise gold tick. Gold is the color of value and arrival (File 01, §1.2); each completed step is a small earned thing, and the gold marks accumulate as the visitor advances, so the constellation slowly warms with gold from left to right as the brief takes shape. This accumulation is felt, not announced — the visitor sees their progress becoming gold.
- **Upcoming steps** rest in a quieted Graphite — present, legible, and clearly "not yet," with no alarm and no graying-out.

Connecting the six labels is a single thin **connecting line** that fills progressively in the **Signature Gradient** as steps complete — violet at the journey's start, warming toward gold as the visitor nears Review. This is the brand's soul (File 01) made into a progress bar: the line literally carries the visitor from the violet of *the spark of the new* at Project toward the gold of *arrival* at the final step. The fill advances by a smooth **Bloom** of gradient light extending into the next segment each time a step completes — never a jump, never a snapped percentage.

The constellation is **interactive within the rules of the journey**: a visitor may tap or click any *completed* step's label to travel back to it directly (their answers intact), exactly as the Review step's edit-jumps do (§14.8). Upcoming steps are not yet reachable from the constellation — they rest disabled in quieted Graphite (File 10, §10.7) — because the visitor reaches them by completing the conversation in order, the way a real brief unfolds. The constellation thus does double duty: it is both the map and the means of stepping back through the map.

### 14.1.4 — The Autosave Reassurance

Resting near the progress constellation is a quiet Geist Mono **autosave micro-label** — a calm, low-key line confirming the brief is held (a steady, unhurried "Your brief is saved" in quieted Graphite, the words it carries are fixed canon only in spirit, not wording, but it always reads as plain reassurance). It is never a loud "Saved!" toast that pops and demands notice; it simply *is there*, a steady fact of the room, so the visitor never fears losing their work. When a new answer is committed and the brief updates, the micro-label gives the faintest, slowest violet **Pulse** — a single calm breath of acknowledgment — and settles. This is the only visible sign of the autosave, and it is enough: the visitor learns within the first step that the room is holding everything for them, and they relax. (The underlying persistence to localStorage is fixed backend, File 18; the visitor never sees the mechanism, only the calm reassurance that it works.)

### 14.1.5 — The Navigation Controls (Back · Skip · Continue)

At the base of every station sits the same calm trio of controls, in the same place, so the visitor learns the room's hands once:

- **Continue** — the **primary button** (File 10, §10.4.1), filled with the **Signature Gradient**, label in Archive White Geist Mono, sitting at the right. It is the one thing the room most wants the visitor to do at this station: finish here and travel onward. Its label reads as a calm forward verb appropriate to the step (a plain "Continue" through the middle steps). On the final station it becomes **Send brief** (§14.8). When a required field is not yet satisfied, Continue rests in its calm disabled state (File 10, §10.7) — a quieted Graphite with its gradient withheld — and a quiet Geist Mono micro-label beside it states exactly what is still needed (for example, naming the one required field awaiting an answer), so the path forward is never a mystery and the visitor never taps a dead button wondering why.
- **Back** — the **secondary button** (File 10, §10.4.2), a thin violet outline pill, sitting at the left. It is always present from the second station onward (it is absent only on the first station, Project, where there is nowhere behind to go), and it always returns the visitor to the prior station with every answer intact. Back is treated with exactly as much care and light as Continue — the door back is never quieter than the door forward.
- **Skip** — a **tertiary / text action** (File 10, §10.4.3), the quietest tier, a small violet Geist Mono link sitting near Continue, present **only on stations whose fields are entirely optional** (Vision, Style, Structure, Budget — every step except Project, which holds required fields, and Review, which is the summary). Skip lets a visitor who wants to brief fast move past an optional station without answering, and its label reads plainly as a gentle "Skip this for now." Skipping a station still marks it complete in the constellation (a skipped station is a *decided* station — the visitor chose to leave it blank), so the gold confirmation and the gradient fill still advance; the visitor's forward progress is never penalized for traveling light. A skipped station can always be returned to later from the constellation or from Review's edit-jumps to add detail.

The discipline of the trio: **Continue is the brightest thing (gradient), Back is a real and equal alternative (violet outline), Skip is an optional detour (quiet violet text).** The visitor reads the hierarchy of every station's foot at a glance, exactly as the three-tier button system promises (File 10, §10.4).

### 14.1.6 — The Travel Between Stations (The Step Seam)

Movement from one station to the next is never a cut and never a page-reload blank. It is the Observatory's recede-and-arrive travel (File 10, §10.6.4 and §10.10), given here as the **step seam** — a small, witnessed Seam (File 18) inside the Commission Room:

On **Continue** (or Skip), the current station's content recedes a short distance and softens back — a calm layered exit, the fields fading as they drift a hair away from the visitor — while, in the same continuous beat, the next station's content arrives from a hair behind the picture plane and settles forward into place (a **Reveal** into a **Settle**, File 01). The station surface itself holds steady as the persistent frame; only the content within it travels. As the content moves, the progress constellation's violet "you are here" mark **slides** smoothly along the connecting line from the old step to the new one, and the gold confirmation tick lands on the step just left while the gradient fill blooms into the next segment. The whole move is brief and weighty — about one calm beat — so the visitor *witnesses* themselves advancing through the brief. On **Back**, this exact motion reverses: the prior station's content returns from where it receded, the violet mark slides back, and the gradient fill recedes by one segment — so moving backward through the brief *feels* like moving backward, spatially coherent and never disorienting.

Under reduced-motion preference (File 00, File 17), the step seam softens to a gentle, brief cross-fade in which the constellation's violet mark and the gold confirmations still move and accumulate — the *meaning* ("I have completed this and traveled to the next") survives intact without the spatial travel.

## 14.2 — Station 01 · Project

> *The station copy, in spirit: "Let's start with the basics — who you are and what you're building."*

The first station gathers the brief's foundation: **name** (required), **email** (required), **company**, **project type** (required), and **readiness**. This is the only station carrying required fields, and it carries them first because a brief Norvo cannot reply to is no brief at all — the name, email, and project type are how the studio answers.

- **Name** and **email** are **text fields** in the Observatory instrument-grade treatment (File 10, §10.6.1): a clean Archive White slot, a small uppercase tracked-out Geist Mono label above it, a thin Graphite hairline beneath. On focus, the field comes alive in Norvo Violet — the rule warms and brightens, a faint violet luminous edge surrounds it, and a slow violet **Pulse** marks "this is where you are typing." Exactly one field is focused at a time. The two required fields are quietly marked as required by a small Geist Mono indicator in their labels — never an alarming red asterisk, but a calm tracked-out "required" cue within the four-color system. As each is satisfied with a valid entry, it settles to its calm "complete" state with a small violet confirmation mark resting at its end (File 10, §10.6.1).
- **Company** is an identical optional text field, its label carrying no required cue. A visitor with no company simply leaves it; the field never insists.
- **Project type** is a **single-select** choice rendered as the chip language (File 10, §10.6.2) — a row of small pill chips, each a project type, set in Geist Mono. At rest each chip is a thin Graphite outline on Archive White: "available, not yet chosen." On choosing, the selected chip fills with a calm violet wash arriving by a quick gentle **Bloom** from the chip's center, its label warming toward Archive White, a faint violet glow surrounding it — unmistakably "chosen and alive." Because project type is single-select, choosing one chip drains the previously chosen chip's violet by the same Bloom in reverse, so exactly one is ever lit; the visitor always sees their single answer.
- **Readiness** is an optional choice — how ready the visitor is to begin (rendered as the same chip language, single-select, or as a clean toggle/scale of a few Geist Mono poles where the choices are ordered). It is gentle and pressure-free: there is no "wrong" readiness, and the visitor may leave it unanswered.

**Continue** on this station unlocks the instant the three required fields — name, email, project type — hold valid entries; until then it rests calmly disabled with its micro-label naming what remains (File 10, §10.7). There is no **Back** here (this is the first station) and no **Skip** (this station holds required fields). Once Continue is satisfied and pressed, the step seam carries the visitor to Vision, the Project step's gold confirmation lands in the constellation, and the gradient line blooms into its second segment.

**Mobile (Project):** Name, email, and company stack full-width and generously tall, each scrolling itself to a comfortable position clear of the on-screen keyboard on focus so the visitor always sees what they type and the label above it (File 10, §10.6.1). The project-type chips reflow into a comfortable wrapped grid of thumb-sized targets with clear spacing so adjacent chips are never mis-tapped; selection delivers the violet fill and a contained glow from the contact point immediately on finger-down (File 10, §10.6.2). The full-width **Continue** button rests at the bottom within thumb reach, never hidden behind the keyboard; its disabled-state micro-label sits just above it.

## 14.3 — Station 02 · Vision

> *The station copy, in spirit: "Now the why — what this project needs to achieve, and anything else you want us to know."*

The second station is where the brief becomes a conversation about *purpose.* It gathers the project's **goals** (multi-select) and a **free-text** field for anything in the visitor's own words. Every field here is optional — Vision is the first station to carry a **Skip**.

- **Goals** is a **multi-select** set of chips (File 10, §10.6.2) — a calm field of Geist Mono pills, each a possible project goal. Unlike Project's single-select project type, here the visitor may light as many as apply: each chosen chip fills with the violet wash by its own **Bloom** and holds lit; choosing more lights more, so the visitor builds up a small constellation of violet goals that together describe what the project is *for*. Deselecting drains a chip by the reverse Bloom. The chips are calm and unhurried; there is no counter pressuring the visitor toward "enough."
- **The free-text field** is a generous multi-line text field in the instrument-grade treatment — a larger Archive White slot with the same violet-alive focus behavior (warmed rule, luminous edge, the slow Pulse marking where the visitor is writing). Its label and a quiet helper line invite the visitor to say anything in their own voice — the studio reads it. It never demands a minimum; an empty field is a fine answer, and a long one is welcomed equally.

**Continue** here is always available (no required fields), and **Skip** sits quietly beside it for a visitor who wants to brief fast. Whether the visitor fills Vision richly, lightly, or skips it entirely, the step is marked complete in the constellation and the gradient advances. **Back** returns to Project with name, email, and every chip intact.

**Mobile (Vision):** The goal chips reflow into the wrapped thumb-sized grid; the free-text field is full-width and generously tall, scrolling clear of the keyboard on focus and re-flowing the layout calmly as the keyboard arrives (never a jump). The full-width **Continue** sits at the bottom in thumb reach with the quiet **Skip** text action just beneath or beside it, clearly the lighter of the two.

## 14.4 — Station 03 · Style

> *The station copy, in spirit: "Let's talk about how it should feel — the look, the brand, the references in your head."*

The third station is the most expressive: it gathers the project's **vibe words**, **branding status**, a **brand-file upload**, **colours**, and **reference links**. This is where the visitor hands Norvo their *taste* — and the station is the warmest, most invitational of the six, because taste is personal and the room must make it safe to share. Every field is optional; Style carries a **Skip**.

- **Vibe words** is a **multi-select** chip field (File 10, §10.6.2) of evocative single words describing how the project should feel, rendered in the same Bloom-to-violet selection language as Goals. The visitor lights the words that resonate, building a small lit cluster that captures the project's mood. (Where the experience invites the visitor to add their own word beyond the offered set, it does so through a quiet text-entry chip that, once filled, joins the cluster as a lit violet chip identical to the rest — so a custom vibe word lives exactly like a chosen one.)
- **Branding status** is a **single-select** choice — whether the visitor has existing branding, is starting fresh, or is somewhere between — rendered as single-select chips (exactly one lit). The visitor's answer here gently contextualizes the brand-file upload below it.
- **The brand-file upload** is the **file-upload affordance** (File 10, §10.6.3): at rest a calm bordered region with a thin Graphite dashed-feeling hairline frame, a small Geist Mono instruction, and a quiet violet affordance to choose a file. On desktop drag-over, the frame warms to violet and a faint violet **Bloom** fills the region from its center as the instruction updates to a calm "release to add." On drop or choose, a calm processing treatment appears (File 10, §10.8) and, on success, the file is represented as a small Geist Mono row carrying its name with a quiet violet confirmation mark and a tertiary "remove" action. No file ever vanishes without acknowledgment; every added and removed file is witnessed. The upload is optional — a visitor with no brand file simply leaves it.
- **Colours** lets the visitor express preferred colours for their project. This is the one place in the Commission Room where colours *other than the four* may be shown — but only ever as **the visitor's own content**, the colours of *their* future site, never as a restyle of the Norvo interface. The visitor's chosen colours are presented as small calm swatches set within the Observatory's Geist Mono and Archive White chrome; the four-color discipline governs the *room*, while the swatches are the visitor's material laid on the room's table. (Designers: the room's surfaces, type, labels, and controls remain strictly Graphite / Archive White / Norvo Violet / Observatory Gold; only the visitor-supplied colour swatches themselves carry other hues, clearly framed as the visitor's input.) Colours is optional.
- **Reference links** is a calm field for URLs of sites or work the visitor admires — a text field that accepts one link and, on commit, settles it into a small Geist Mono row with a quiet violet confirmation and a tertiary "remove," ready to accept the next. The visitor builds a short, witnessed list of references exactly as files settle into rows in the upload. Optional throughout.

**Continue** is always available; **Skip** is present; **Back** returns to Vision with every chip, file, swatch, and link intact.

**Mobile (Style):** Vibe-word and branding-status chips reflow into the wrapped thumb grid. The brand-file upload becomes a single generous tap affordance that opens the device's own picker — there is no drag-over stage on touch; the tap-to-choose path is the whole interaction (File 10, §10.6.3), and the received-file row with its violet confirmation behaves identically. The colours swatches and reference-link rows stack full-width and comfortably spaced. Every text entry scrolls clear of the keyboard on focus. The full-width **Continue** and quiet **Skip** sit at the bottom in thumb reach.

## 14.5 — Station 04 · Structure

> *The station copy, in spirit: "What the site needs to contain — the pages, the features, and any notes on how it should work."*

The fourth station turns from feeling to *substance*: it gathers the project's **pages** (multi-select), **features** (multi-select), and **notes** (free text). This is the most architectural station — the visitor is sketching the shape of the thing — and it uses the chip language at its fullest. Every field is optional; Structure carries a **Skip**.

- **Pages** is a **multi-select** chip field of the pages the visitor's site might hold (home, about, services, shop, contact, and the like). The visitor lights the pages their project needs, building a lit set that is, fittingly, a small floor-plan of their future site rendered in violet chips. Selection and deselection follow the standard Bloom-to-violet language.
- **Features** is a parallel **multi-select** chip field of capabilities the site might need (a shop, a booking flow, a blog, animations, a contact form, and so on). The two chip fields sit as two calm clusters — *what pages* and *what powers* — so the visitor reads their site's shape and its abilities as two distinct, complementary answers.
- **Notes** is a generous multi-line **free-text** field in the instrument-grade treatment, identical in behavior to Vision's free-text field, inviting any structural detail in the visitor's own words.

**Continue** is always available; **Skip** is present; **Back** returns to Style with every selection intact.

**Mobile (Structure):** Both chip clusters reflow into wrapped thumb-sized grids, clearly separated by their labels so *pages* and *features* never blur together. The notes field is full-width and keyboard-aware. Full-width **Continue** and quiet **Skip** rest at the bottom in thumb reach.

## 14.6 — Station 05 · Budget

> *The station copy, in spirit: "The practical part — your budget, your timeline, and how you found us."*

The fifth station is the last before Review, and it gathers the brief's grounding facts: **budget range**, **timeline**, and **how-heard**. Money can be the most uncomfortable question in a commission, so this station is the calmest and least pressured of all — the questions are presented as gentle, pressure-free ranges, never as a demand for an exact figure. Every field is optional; Budget carries a **Skip**.

- **Budget range** is a **single-select** choice of calm ranges rendered as chips (or as an ordered set of Geist Mono poles where a range scale reads more naturally), exactly one lit. The ranges are stated plainly and without judgment; there is no "too small" framing, and the visitor may always leave it unanswered. The lit range glows in the standard calm violet.
- **Timeline** is a **single-select** choice of when the visitor hopes to begin or launch — the same chip language, exactly one lit.
- **How-heard** is a **single-select** choice of how the visitor found Norvo — the same chip language. It is the lightest, most casual question in the room, and it closes the conversational arc warmly: the brief ends by asking how the visitor arrived, the way a host might at the end of a good first meeting.

**Continue** here carries the visitor to Review and is always available; **Skip** is present; **Back** returns to Structure intact. As the visitor leaves Budget, the constellation's gradient line nears its full warm-gold completion — by design, the visitor *sees* the brand's soul resolve toward gold exactly as they approach arrival, foreshadowing the gold-touched success to come (File 10, §10.6.4).

**Mobile (Budget):** All three choices render as wrapped thumb-sized chip grids, clearly labeled and separated. The full-width **Continue** (its label here still "Continue," carrying the visitor to Review) and quiet **Skip** sit at the bottom in thumb reach.

## 14.7 — The Sixth Station · Review (The Commission Confirmed)

> *The station copy, in spirit: "Here's everything you've told us. Look it over, change anything, and send it to the studio."*

The sixth and final station is the quiet climax of the Commission Room — the moment the brief becomes whole and the visitor commits it to Norvo. Where the prior five stations *gathered*, Review **reflects**: it presents a calm, read-only summary of every answer the visitor has given, grouped by section, so the visitor sees their entire brief at once, as the studio will see it. This is the act of confirmation, and it is treated with the dignity of a signature.

### 14.7.1 — The Summary

The visitor's answers are presented as five calm grouped panels — **Project, Vision, Style, Structure, Budget** — in canon order, each a quiet Observatory surface (File 10, §10.5) holding that section's answers as read-only Geist Mono in the Luminous Readout treatment. Labels are small, uppercase, tracked-out; the visitor's own answers sit beside them in clean, perfectly aligned Geist Mono (the monospace's native column alignment making the summary read as *engineered and exact*, File 10, §10.1.2). Chosen chips appear as small lit-violet tags; the brand file appears as its named row; colours appear as the visitor's swatches; references and free text appear as the visitor wrote them. An answer the visitor left blank or skipped is shown plainly as a calm "not specified" in quieted Graphite — never an error, never a gap that alarms; a skipped section is simply a section the visitor chose to leave open, and Review states it without judgment.

### 14.7.2 — The Edit-Jumps

Each of the five grouped panels carries a quiet **"edit" affordance** — a **secondary action** (File 10, §10.4.2), a thin violet outline) — that jumps the visitor directly back to that exact station with every answer intact (File 10, §10.6.4). The jump uses the same step-seam travel in reverse: the Review summary recedes, the chosen station arrives from a hair behind the picture plane and settles, the constellation's violet "you are here" mark slides back to it, and the visitor edits in the live station exactly as before. Committing the edit (a **Continue** that now reads as "Back to Review," or simply advancing forward through the constellation) travels the visitor forward to Review again, their change reflected. The edit-jump is the same mechanism the progress constellation offers (§14.1.3) — Review simply places an obvious door to it beside each section, so the visitor never hunts for how to change one answer.

### 14.7.3 — The Send

At the foot of the Review station sits the single **primary button** of the entire Commission Room's climax — **Send brief** — filled with the **Signature Gradient** (File 10, §10.4.1), the one gradient-filled action in the view, carrying the faint slow shimmer of the brand's soul at rest. It is the brightest, warmest object on the station: the visitor knows at a glance that this is the act the whole room was built toward. There is at most one primary button in the view, and this is it.

On press, the button does not vanish into a disconnected spinner. It holds its place and its label calmly updates to the active verb (a quiet "Sending…"), while a faint violet **Pulse** breathes along its edge — the thing the visitor pressed is the thing that shows the work (File 10, §10.8). The send is the fixed backend act: the brief is dispatched to Norvo as an email (File 18). The visitor sees only the calm, lit "this is working," never a frozen screen and never a jarring blank.

**Back** remains present on Review (returning to Budget), so even at the threshold of sending, the door back is lit. Review carries **no Skip** — there is nothing to skip at the summary; there is only to confirm or to step back and refine.

### 14.7.4 — The Success State (Arrival)

When the send completes, the Commission Room resolves into its success state — the **emotional landing of the entire site**, the moment the visitor's commitment is received and honored. The "Sending…" button settles, without a jolt, into the success state (File 10, §10.8): the Review summary calmly recedes and softens back, and in its place arrives — by a gentle **Reveal** into a **Settle** — the success message, set centered in the Luminous Readout with abundant calm space around it.

The message reads exactly, and only, **"Brief received — thank you."** (Fixed canon copy, File 18 — never reworded, never punctuated differently.) It is set in Geist Mono in the harmonious hierarchy, and the **Signature Gradient** shimmers slowly across the words — violet flowing into gold, the resolved chord (File 01, §1.2; File 10, §10.1.2) — the one place in the Commission Room where the brand's soul appears in full, calm glory, because this is the room's true arrival. A faint **Observatory Gold** warmth touches the moment: the gold of *a finished thing*, of value received, of a commission accepted. The success state never erupts in celebration — no confetti, no fanfare, no exclamation. It is the quiet, warm, certain note of a studio that has received a serious brief and will reply. The whole journey from Project to here resolves into poise.

The success state is never a dead end (File 10, §10.9). Beneath the message sit calm, obvious next doors as quiet secondary and tertiary actions: a route to **view the work** (Projects) and the standing route **Home**, plus the always-present navigation and footer. A visitor who has just sent a brief is gently shown where to go next — to see Norvo's work while they wait for the studio's reply — and is never stranded on a terminal screen. The autosave reassurance, having done its work, settles quiet; the brief is sent, and the room is at peace.

### 14.7.5 — If the Send Cannot Complete

If the send cannot complete (a connection lost mid-dispatch), the room does **not** alarm, flash, or discard the brief (File 10, §10.6.1, error grammar; File 17). The button settles from "Sending…" back to a calm ready state, and a small, plainly worded Geist Mono message arrives beneath it by a gentle **Reveal**, stating in calm language exactly what happened and that the brief is safe and held, inviting the visitor to send again. Because every answer persists (§14.1.4), nothing is lost; the visitor's entire brief waits intact, and a second **Send brief** is one calm tap away. The error never uses a fifth color — it is rendered in the deepened Graphite-violet weight of the error grammar (File 10, §10.6.1) — and it never blocks the visitor from seeing or editing their brief.

## 14.8 — The Complete Mobile Re-Choreography of the Commission Room

Mobile is first-class, not a fallback (File 00, §0.6). The Commission Room is re-choreographed for the phone with equal intention — every station, control, and transition has its exact thumb-and-keyboard equivalent. The inline mobile notes in §§14.2–14.6 specify each station; this section specifies the room's mobile spine as a whole, so the phone experience is coherent end to end.

### 14.8.1 — The Mobile Progress Constellation

The six-label progress constellation condenses, on the phone, into a compact **"Step N of 6"** Geist Mono readout paired with a slim **Signature-Gradient progress line** that fills as steps complete (File 10, §10.6.4) — violet warming toward gold across the six, exactly as the desktop connecting line. This keeps "Where am I? How far remains?" answerable at a glance without the six full labels crowding the small canvas. A **tap on the readout** expands it into the full six named steps — Project · Vision · Style · Structure · Budget · Review — overlaid as a calm vertical or wrapped list, each completed step carrying its gold confirmation and each tappable to travel back, the current step in violet with its Pulse, upcoming steps in quieted Graphite. The visitor can thus still see and jump the whole map on the phone; the map is condensed, never amputated. A tap elsewhere, or on the expanded readout again, collapses it back to the compact form.

### 14.8.2 — The Mobile Station and Controls

Each station's fields stack **full-width** in a single comfortable column with generous spacing, so every field and chip is an unambiguous thumb target (File 10, §10.6). Text fields are generously tall and scroll themselves to a comfortable position clear of the on-screen keyboard on focus, with their label visible above; the keyboard's arrival re-flows the content above it calmly, never with a jump (File 10, §10.6.1). Chip fields reflow into wrapped grids of thumb-sized pills with clear spacing so adjacent chips are never mis-tapped, and selection delivers the violet **Bloom** and a contained glow from the contact point immediately on finger-down. The brand-file upload becomes the single tap-to-pick affordance with no drag-over stage (File 10, §10.6.3).

The navigation controls re-lay for the thumb: the **Continue** primary button is **full-width at the bottom of every station, always within thumb reach and never hidden behind the keyboard** (File 10, §10.6.4). **Back** (the violet outline secondary) and **Skip** (the quiet violet text action, on the optional stations) sit clearly but lighter than Continue — Back as a comfortable secondary tap target, Skip as the quietest tier — so the hierarchy of the three doors reads on the phone exactly as on the desktop. Continue's disabled-state micro-label sits just above it on Station 01 so the visitor reads what is still needed without scrolling.

### 14.8.3 — Mobile Step Travel, Review, and Success

Step-to-step travel uses the **same recede-and-arrive step seam** (§14.1.6), re-timed to the smaller canvas — the current station's stacked content recedes and softens as the next arrives from a hair behind and settles; the compact "Step N of 6" advances and the gradient line fills; **Back** reverses it exactly. The persistent frosted top navigation bar holds while station content travels beneath it (File 10, §10.10).

On mobile, **Review** stacks its five grouped summary panels vertically full-width, each with its quiet violet **edit** affordance as a comfortable tap target that jumps back to that station; the full-width **Send brief** primary button (Signature Gradient) rests at the bottom in thumb reach, with **Back** present and lighter. The "Sending…" processing state and the **"Brief received — thank you."** success message behave identically to desktop, re-proportioned to the small canvas: the success message and its gradient shimmer center full-width with abundant space, and the calm next-door actions (view the work, Home) stack centered and full-width within the thumb's reach, so the visitor who has just sent a brief is never stranded at the bottom of a phone screen (File 10, §10.9).

### 14.8.4 — Reduced-Motion on Mobile and Desktop Alike

Under a reduced-motion preference (File 00, §0.6; File 17), the Commission Room degrades gracefully into a calmer, equally legible, equally navigable form on every device. The step seam and edit-jumps soften into gentle, brief cross-fades; the constellation's violet "you are here" mark and the gold completion marks still move and accumulate, and the gradient progress line still fills — so the *meaning* of "I have completed this step and traveled to the next" survives intact without the spatial travel. The autosave Pulse becomes a calm steady warmth; the processing button's edge Pulse becomes a steady glow; the success-state shimmer settles to a steady gradient wash. The visitor in reduced-motion completes the entire six-step brief, sees their progress, edits any answer, sends, and receives **"Brief received — thank you."** with no loss of clarity, dignity, or wayfinding.

## 14.9 — How the Commission Room Holds the Covenant and the Canon

The Intake is the site's most consequential page, so it is held to every rule at once. If a team is ever unsure whether a moment in the Commission Room belongs, run it against this list:

1. **Are all six steps present, in canon order — Project → Vision → Style → Structure → Budget → Review — with every fixed field intact** (Project: name\*, email\*, company, project type\*, readiness; Vision: goals, free text; Style: vibe words, branding status, brand-file upload, colours, reference links; Structure: pages, features, notes; Budget: budget range, timeline, how-heard; Review: read-only summary, per-section edit-jumps, send)?
2. **Does the visitor always know where they are in the six and how far remains** — the progress constellation (violet current, gold completed, quieted-Graphite upcoming, Signature-Gradient fill) answering at a glance on every device?
3. **Is Back always as lit as Continue, and is Skip present on exactly the optional stations** (Vision, Style, Structure, Budget) and never on Project or Review?
4. **Does the visitor never fear losing their work** — the quiet, constant autosave reassurance, and a send-failure that keeps the brief safe and held?
5. **Is every transition between stations a witnessed Seam** — the recede-and-arrive step seam, reversing exactly on Back — with no cut, no flash, no teleport?
6. **Are only the four colors used in their Observatory roles** — Graphite architecture, Archive White interior light, Norvo Violet the living signal of focus and selection, Observatory Gold the value of each completed step and the warmth of arrival — with the Signature Gradient reserved for the progress line, the Continue/Send-brief primary action, and the success state, and never sprayed across the room? (The sole exception: the visitor's own colour swatches in Style, clearly framed as *their* content, never a restyle of the room.)
7. **Is the type Geist Mono in the Luminous Readout treatment** throughout — instrument-grade labels, perfectly aligned answers, the harmonious hierarchy, the shimmer reserved for the success words?
8. **Is the success message exactly "Brief received — thank you."** — never reworded — and is the success state a warm, calm arrival with lit next-doors, never a dead end and never a celebration that erupts?
9. **Is the Usability Covenant intact at every step** — the persistent navigation and footer always reaching Home, Projects, Services, Contact, and Start a Project; the visitor never trapped in the brief; the route to contact and to the work always one obvious action away?
10. **Does the entire room have its precise, equal mobile re-choreography** — condensed constellation, full-width keyboard-aware stations, thumb-sized chips, bottom-anchored full-width Continue, tap-to-pick upload, re-timed step travel, and the identical success arrival — never "simplified for mobile"?

Ten yeses, or it is not the Commission Room.
