### Places (Named Locations Within the World)
| Name | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **The Observatory** | The one inhabited Place — see "The Journey & Its Eras" above. Norvo's future *architecture* of the web; the interior the visitor arrives in, stands within, and acts from at the summit of The Ascent. It is a *building you are inside.* (Listed here as well so every named location lives in one table.) |
| **The Spaceport** | **Scenery, never a destination.** The believable-future vista seen *beyond* The Observatory's windows (File 09) — the world outside the building. The relationship is fixed: **The Observatory is the interior the visitor inhabits; The Spaceport is the exterior the visitor only ever beholds through The Observatory's windows.** It is rendered, like everything, in the four colors (Graphite structure against Archive White light, the faintest Norvo Violet signal and Observatory Gold warmth in its far points) and never with any literal sci-fi spacecraft, launch gantry, rocket, or cockpit imagery — it is *future architecture seen at distance,* the same believable-future register as The Observatory itself, just farther away. > **The Spaceport boundary rule:** The Spaceport is atmosphere and horizon only. It must never grow into a second navigable environment, a "next level," an explorable exterior, or a place the visitor is invited to travel to. If a moment tempts the team to *go to* The Spaceport, that moment is wrong — the journey ends *inside* The Observatory, looking out. The Spaceport's entire job is to make The Observatory feel like a real building in a real future.

## 18.2 — Terminology Glossary

Definitions of working terms used throughout the bible.

- **Environment (as in "websites as environments"):** A website that communicates through space, movement, progression, atmosphere, and storytelling — a *place* you travel through — rather than a stack of flat sections. Norvo's core thesis.
- **Era:** One of the five distinct atmospheres/temperaments of The Ascent. Eras are *traveled through,* not jumped between.
- **Temperament:** How the universal system (font, color, motion, space, light) is tuned for a given era. The five temperaments: austere, composed, operational, immersive, harmonious.
- **The Ascent:** See Naming Canon. The homepage journey through the five eras.
- **Seam:** A gradual, witnessed transition between two eras. Never a hard cut.
- **The Time Warp:** The one climactic Seam (Era 4 → Future). The peak.
- **The Observatory:** See Naming Canon. The inhabited future Place — the building the visitor arrives inside at the summit of The Ascent.
- **The Spaceport:** See Naming Canon (Places). The believable-future vista seen *beyond* The Observatory's windows — the world outside the building. Scenery only: beheld through the glass, never entered, never navigated into, holding no content or affordances. It exists to give The Observatory the depth and permanence of a real place in a real future. The visitor's relationship to it is purely visual — *The Observatory is inhabited; The Spaceport is only beheld.*
- **Color arc:** The gradual entry and rebalancing of the four colors across the eras (monochrome → spark → touch → signal/value → light → harmony).
- **Signature Gradient:** The violet-to-gold 120° gradient; the brand's soul.
- **Trinity of intent:** Depth, motion, intention.
- **Live (badge):** A real, shipped client project.
- **Prototype (badge):** An in-house interactive demo; a walled-off fictional brand universe.
- **Featured (badge):** A highlighted project entry.
- **Walled-off universe:** A prototype world (Aurora, Strata) with its own branding, exempt from the Norvo restyle.
- **The Usability Covenant:** The five always-answerable questions guaranteeing the visitor is never lost.
- **First-class mobile:** The principle that mobile is fully re-choreographed (not simplified) with equal intention.
- **Re-choreographed (for mobile):** Deliberately re-designed for touch and small screens — exact gestures, layout shifts, re-timing, depth adaptation — never merely "simplified."
- **Reduced-motion variant:** The calmer, equally legible and navigable form of any moment, honored when a visitor prefers reduced motion.
- **The Bloom / Settle / Drift / Parallax / Reveal / Shift / Warp / Pulse:** The eight named motions (File 01 §1.3).

## 18.3 — Do / Do-Not Guardrails

### DO
- **DO** keep Geist Mono as the only font, everywhere, always.
- **DO** use only the four colors, in their per-era roles (File 01 §1.2).
- **DO** make every transition gradual and witnessed; describe the Seam into whatever comes next.
- **DO** let color enter gradually across the eras — begin near-monochrome, end in four-color harmony.
- **DO** keep the Usability Covenant intact in every section — the visitor always knows where they are, how to navigate, how to contact, how to start a project, how to view work.
- **DO** treat mobile as first-class: specify exact gestures, layout shifts, re-timing, and depth adaptation for every interaction.
- **DO** describe motion by named behavior, feel, and easing character — never by code.
- **DO** justify every element by depth, motion, or intention.
- **DO** keep The Observatory as believable future *architecture* (research facility / command center / design lab / future archive).
- **DO** keep The Spaceport as distant scenery only — the lit future world beheld through The Observatory's windows, giving the place depth and permanence.
- **DO** respect the prototype walls — Aurora and Strata are their own universes.
- **DO** write with one-interpretation precision — concrete, observable description; exact motion, timing feel, spatial relationships, color roles.
- **DO** keep the voice immersive, spatial, restrained, and certain.

### DO NOT
- **DO NOT** introduce any fifth color, any second font, or any font swap between eras/pages.
- **DO NOT** write code, CSS, component APIs, framework/library names, or feasibility/implementation/performance notes anywhere in the bible.
- **DO NOT** make anything snap or cut abruptly between eras — no hard transitions.
- **DO NOT** let storytelling override usability; immersion is never a trap.
- **DO NOT** make the experience a game, sci-fi/Star Wars piece, fake operating system, interactive movie, tech demo, Awwwards experiment, virtual world, or metaverse.
- **DO NOT** render literal retro parody (no GeoCities, Win95, skeuomorphic kitsch). Evoke each epoch's essence through the Norvo system.
- **DO NOT** make The Observatory outer space, a spaceship interior, or a movie set.
- **DO NOT** turn The Spaceport into a destination, a navigable exterior, a "next level," or a literal rocket / launch-pad / cockpit scene — it is seen through the glass, never entered, and never shows literal spacecraft.
- **DO NOT** use bounce, overshoot, springiness, flashing, or startling motion/sound.
- **DO NOT** invent new major pages or rename existing ones, badges, services, intake steps, or copy that is fixed canon.
- **DO NOT** restyle the prototypes (Aurora, Strata) to match Norvo's palette/font.
- **DO NOT** write "simplified for mobile" — always describe the exact mobile re-choreography.
- **DO NOT** add elements that exist only because they look cool.
- **DO NOT** reference any existing or current implementation — this is a clean-slate vision.
- **DO NOT** use vague adjectives where observable description is possible (no "modern," "sleek," "dynamic," "engaging" standing alone).

## 18.4 — Team Handoff: What Each Discipline Owns

The bible is the shared source; each discipline owns a slice of turning it into reality. All disciplines are bound by File 00, File 01, and this file.

- **Creative Director:** Owns the canon and its integrity. Guards the thesis, the story spine, the four-color arc, and the Usability Covenant. Final arbiter when files seem to conflict (earlier file wins). Ensures every era still serves "websites as environments" and "WE DESIGN THE FUTURE." Approves any new proper noun and records it in this file.
- **UX / Information Architecture:** Owns the Usability Covenant in practice — wayfinding, the persistent navigation, the location sense across eras, the skip/return paths, the five always-answerable questions, and graceful degradation. Ensures the Intake's six steps and the Contact channel function exactly as fixed. Owns loading/empty/error states (File 10).
- **Visual / Brand Designers:** Own the per-era expression of the four-color arc and the Geist Mono treatment (File 01 §1.1–1.2), the spacing/grid loosening (§1.4), the material/light (§1.5), the monogram/wordmark, and the badge system. Keep the system to four colors and one font.
- **Motion Designers:** Own the Motion Vocabulary (the eight named motions), every Seam, the Time Warp choreography (File 07), and the reduced-motion variants. Ensure no bounce, no teleporting, every transition witnessed, motion always serving story or action.
- **3D / Environment Artists:** Own the depth, atmosphere, and architecture of Era 4 (Experiences) and The Observatory — volume, light, space, the believable future *architecture.* Keep The Observatory as a building, never a spaceship; keep The Spaceport as distant future scenery beheld through the windows, never an enterable place; keep depth in service of the story, never a tech demo.
- **Developers / Engineers:** Own making reality match the words — the depth, motion, responsiveness, performance, accessibility, and the fixed backends (Intake autosave + email submit; Contact email; localStorage). They translate vision into build; they do not redefine vision. (The bible deliberately gives them no implementation prescriptions — that authority is theirs, within the experience the bible describes.)
- **UX Writers / Copy:** Own the voice (immersive, spatial, restrained, certain) and every word the visitor reads — within fixed copy (brand statement, supporting lines, service taglines, "Brief received — thank you," etc.). Keep the tone declarative, never hyped, never cute.
- **Sound Designers:** Own the optional sound layer (File 01 §1.6) — atmosphere over music, the per-era evolution, the Time Warp's single sonic peak, the always-available mute, and full equality of the silent experience.
- **Accessibility Lead (cross-cutting):** Ensures every discipline's work honors reduced-motion, keyboard/assistive paths, text equivalents, and graceful degradation — the Usability Covenant for everyone (File 00).

## 18.5 — Conflict Resolution & Change Control

- **Authority order:** Company Truths → Guardrails → File 00 → File 01 → File 18 → individual files. Earlier always wins.
- **New names:** Any new proper noun must be approved by the Creative Director and recorded in §18.1 before use anywhere. No team coins names independently.
- **Fixed canon is fixed:** The era names, The Observatory, The Spaceport, the Time Warp, the four colors, Geist Mono, the page structure, the services, the intake steps, the fixed copy, and the project entities are immutable. Everything else is open vision space — described in the bible, refined by the teams, but never in violation of the fixed canon or the covenant.
